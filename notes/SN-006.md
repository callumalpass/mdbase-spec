**Sections:** §5.11, §7.2, §12.1
**Ambiguity:** The spec says defaults are applied to the "effective" value and "SHOULD omit fields that were filled solely by defaults" on write. But the test format's `expect.frontmatter` checks the effective (read-time) result which includes defaults. There's a subtle difference between what's on disk and what's returned.
**Assumption:** `expect.frontmatter` checks the effective frontmatter (defaults applied). `expect.frontmatter_written` / `expect.frontmatter_not_written` check what was actually persisted to disk.
**Resolution:** §14.3.1 "Extended Test Format" added to 14-conformance.md, documenting `frontmatter_written` and `frontmatter_not_written` as extended assertion fields.
