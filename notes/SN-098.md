**Sections:** §13.2, §14.6
**Issue:** Two pieces of non-normative guidance are stated with more confidence than warranted.

- **§13.2**: `"For small collections (< 100 files), caching overhead may not be worthwhile. For large collections (1000+ files), caching is strongly recommended."` — `"strongly recommended"` is informal; use `SHOULD` or qualify the thresholds as rough guidelines that vary by hardware and implementation.
- **§14.6 Performance table**: Latency targets (`< 10ms`, `< 100ms`, etc.) are asserted without qualification. Worth a note that these are illustrative targets, not hard requirements, since they depend on hardware and runtime.
